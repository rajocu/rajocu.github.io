Protect Your Online Assets

18 Sep 2016, 11:03

Cyber theft is a headache we shouldn’t have to deal with. It takes time and energy away from our families our jobs and our hobbies. It also drives costs for everyone since companies simply pass on the losses to consumers.

Here are a few simple things you can do to protect yourself:

1) Enable 2FA (2 factor authentication). This prevents your online accounts from being accessed even if your password is compromised. You can read more about 2FA here: https://ssd.eff.org/en/module/how-enable-two-factor-authentication
- At a minimum, I recommend 2FA for the following types of online accounts:

  • Bank accounts
  • Email accounts (Gmail, yahoo, office365, etc.)
  • Social Networks (Facebook, LinkedIn, etc.)

2) Use a different password for each site. I realize this becomes a hassle so I recommend using a password manager. My personal favorite is 1Password.

3) Enable WPA2 encryption from your wi-fi router.

4) Check your credit report twice a year at minimum. If you think you are too busy, consider a credit monitoring service.
