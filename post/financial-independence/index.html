A Path to Financial Independence for Citizens of Developed Countries

I wanted to write about financial independence because I believe it is an easier goal to attain then most believe. More importantly, I believe that everyone that reaches it, will be in a position to impact humanity in a positive way.

The Goal

Be in a position where you don’t need to labor over something that doesn’t make you happy but instead to be free to focus on the things that truly matter!

The Ask

Put together a plan, be disciplined and be patient

So How do I get there?

  1. Calculate all your debt including:
    1. Credit card debts
    2. Student loans
    3. Car debt (total lease payments OR balance on loan)
    4. Rent or Mortgage
    5. Other debt
    6. List your debt in order of cost i.e. those with the highest interest rates and fees and place mortgage and student loans near the bottom.
    7. [ ] link to spreadsheet or sites like mint.com
  2. Come up with a budget
    • Click here for a sample budget:
  3. From the budget above, multiply your monthly budget by six.
    • This is your short-term target. 6-months living expenses in savings.
  4. Pay the minimum on every account except the one that you listed at the top.
    • Pay as much as you can every month to the account costing you the most in interest and fees. Once that account reaches a balance of zero, move it to the bottom and start over with the new one at the top.
  5. Rule of thumbs to live by:
    • Car payment <= 10% of take home pay
    • Mortgage/Rent <= 36% of take home pay
    • 401K contribution = 100% of employer match
    • Savings >= 10%, start lower if it’s out of reach for now and increase by 1% each year.
